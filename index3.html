2) Thermosetting

Main properties of Thermosetting

  • Chemical Change
  • Rigidity
  • Heat Resistance

Chemical Change (Curing)

Thermosetting materials or thermo-hardening materials; these materials undergo a chemical change when they are subjected to heat and pressure. After this change, they cannot be changed by further application of heat and pressure.

Rigidity

These materials cure within the heated mold, so they gain rigidity before the applied pressure and temperature is removed. Once hardened and set, they are stronger and harder than thermoplastics.

Examples of Thermosetting

The melamine formaldehyde and Bakelite are common examples of these materials, known for their high thermal stability and strength.
